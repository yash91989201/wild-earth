# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

WildEarth serves luxury wildlife travelers planning India safari trips across national parks, lodges, itineraries, and photography-led experiences. They are likely choosing between destinations, validating operator trust, and deciding whether to start a direct inquiry before they know exact permits, jeep availability, or travel logistics.

## Product Purpose

WildEarth exists to turn high-intent safari interest into qualified direct inquiries. The site should help travelers understand where to go, what kind of trip fits them, and why booking through verified on-ground teams is safer, clearer, and more expert than working through opaque intermediaries.

Success means visitors feel enough confidence, specificity, and premium care to contact WildEarth through WhatsApp or the planning form with realistic preferences.

## Brand Personality

Cultivated, grounded, expert.

The voice should feel premium without resort theater, practical without becoming transactional, and conservation-aware without moral pressure. WildEarth should sound like an experienced field operator who knows parks, permits, guides, lodges, and guest expectations from the ground up.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic travel agency design: deal-heavy packages, stock vacation polish, vague superlatives, and lead forms that feel like quote traps.

Avoid overdone luxury resort design: gold excess, perfume-ad drama, detached interiors, and polish that hides the terrain, wildlife, and logistics.

Avoid adventure-bro positioning: conquest language, extreme-sports urgency, and loud adrenaline-first visuals.

Avoid NGO campaign tone: guilt-forward conservation messaging that overwhelms the traveler job or makes the experience feel like obligation rather than thoughtful participation.

## Design Principles

1. Show field competence before aspiration. Prove trust through direct operations, permit clarity, park knowledge, and grounded details.
2. Make premium feel tactile and specific. Use imagery, copy, and interaction to evoke terrain, timing, local expertise, and quiet confidence.
3. Reduce inquiry anxiety. Make next steps, response expectations, and booking constraints clear before asking for contact.
4. Let discovery lead conversion. Help travelers compare parks, lodges, itineraries, and photography needs, then make inquiry feel natural.
5. Keep conservation integrated. Present ecosystem care and local partnerships as part of operational quality, not a separate guilt campaign.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG AA as the baseline for contrast, focus visibility, keyboard access, form labels, error states, and readable copy.

Respect reduced-motion preferences. Motion should support orientation and atmosphere, not block comprehension or create discomfort.

Structure copy and content for future multilingual support, especially for international travelers and Indian audiences.
